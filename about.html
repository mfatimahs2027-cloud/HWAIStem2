About This Project

Learn how animals across land, mountains, and sea connect to our world.

Life on Land

Animals on land play a huge role in balancing ecosystems. From predators like wild cats to herbivores like goats, each species helps maintain nature’s cycle.

Life in the Mountains

Mountain animals survive in harsh, cold, rocky environments. Their adaptations help scientists understand evolution, climate, and survival.

How You Can Help

Help Wild Cats

Many wild cat species are endangered due to habitat loss, poaching, and illegal wildlife trade. Endangered species include the Asiatic lion, tigers, leopards, and several small wild cats. Help these cats with the links below:

Help Wild Cats:

  • Felidae Conservation Fund
  • The Wildcat Sanctuary
  • Big Cat Rescue
  • Wild Cat Sanctuaries
  • Humane World Wildlife Rescue
  • Best Big Cat Charities

Help Sharks

Sharks are among the most threatened marine species due to overfishing, habitat loss, and finning. Many species are critically endangered, including hammerheads, angelsharks, and oceanic whitetips. Protecting sharks is essential for healthy oceans.

Help Sharks:

  • Shark Allies
  • Best Shark Charities
  • Nonprofit Shark Charities
  • Shark Angels

Help Goats

Uncontrolled goat populations can damage vegetation, soil, biodiversity, and water quality. Of 576 goat breeds, 19 are extinct and 90 are at risk. Conservation efforts are needed to protect them.

Help Goats:

  • The Old Goats Home
  • Frederick Farm Goats
  • Goats for Good
  • Pet Goats Rescue
